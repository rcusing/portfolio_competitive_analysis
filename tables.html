Portfolio Competitive Analysis


Ingo Oszkinat, http://www.ingooszkinat.nl/, 19 Jan. 2012
Brand
  • Professional: The serif typeface and exclusive use of black and grey indicates formality and seriousness.
  • Muted: The background color is not pure white but somewhat greyish. The text is small and unobtrusive, almost blending into the background but not quite.
  • Direct: His work is front and center, unadorned. The descriptions below are short and to the point.
Overall, the page gives me the impression that this is a person who is proud of his work and confident in his ability to deliver, but reluctant to say too much or be overly showy or flashy.
Functionality

Although concise, he provides enough information about himself to get an idea of his background, linking to external sites for those who need more as well as providing his contact information in a visually salient position.

However, it is unclear that the slideshow is only a teaser, showing only 5 sample images from a total of 12 different projects, each with multiple images.

The gallery page features thumbnail images representing the different projects, which gives a striking look. Hovering over the image washes out the color and shows 2 lines of text with the name of the client and type of work, linking to an individual page for that particular project with more images and description. The concise labels and descriptions in this case makes the information rather vague and lacking in detail.

The project page has the same layout as the main page, but instead of an automatic slideshow, he includes some grey circles under the word "Images" at the bottom right, with one circle blacked in for the current image. It may not be immediately apparent that the images will not change on their own, but the layout is simple enough that it's not a huge problem.
Interactivity
Not much for interactivity here. Hovering over links changes the text color to grey. Hovering over thumbnails on the gallery page provides additional information. However, sometimes hovering over the thumbnail does not do the trick, in which case the viewer needs to click on the image. Individual project pages feature a click-through slideshow.
Navigation
It seems like this main page is all there is. The very observant will notice that links are in black and venture a click on "Work" or "About" in the top navigation. Probably the first click will be on "About," but it turns out that the main page is the about page. Same issue with the gallery page, especially because clicking around the text rather than directly on the text does not lead anywhere.


Mindgruve, http://www.mindgruve.com/, 19 Jan. 2012
Brand
  • Innovative: The self-professed representatives of the "next generation." The page headlines zoom in from off-screen. The background images are blurred, implying motion. This is a company that is on the move.
  • Involved: The examples of blog post titles demonstrate community involvement ("Help us donate") and an interest in joining the discussion about topics related to digital marketing.
  • Informal: Still professional, but not so straight-laced. Sans- serif typeface, subtle use of color amidst mostly white on black text. Informal style of writing and limited use of capital letters.
Overall, I get the impression that this is a marketing agency with people who not only know what they're doing and are up on all the latest, but they are also going to be really fun to work with.
Functionality

The top navigation is where it's at, in terms of the functionality of the page. It's a very thin black bar, which stands out next to the color of the background image, but it doesn't detract from the dramatic effect of the background image.

They give enough information to figure out what they do, how they do it, and how to reach them. I like that the phone numbers are emphasized. Clicking on the logos on the gallery page leads to an individual project page, with a sample image and text description of what they did.

However, the main page itself is not particularly revealing. It seems like just a portal/entryway to give an impression of the brand.
Interactivity
A few interactive elements here, but not that much. Hovering over the thumbnails on the main page shows a mini slideshow with somewhat hectic transitions (same effect on the gallery page, but less hectic). A form for messages on the contact page. Clicking on the white arrows on individual project pages brings up another project.
Navigation

Pretty effective top navigation. The Mindgruve logo on the left sticks around for navigating back to the homepage.

It may be fairly natural to click on the thumbnails on the main page or gallery page, but a lot would be lost on the off- chance that the opportunity was missed. When you make it to the project presentation/slideshow, there are nice big left and right arrows to indicate that you can continue looking at different projects. However, no indication of how many pages there are or what's coming up next.
